Welcome To Simple Workout Plans!

On this website, I will detail simple workout plans that will allow you to make 
    good gains in the gym and that wont take you too long while still being extremely effective.
    I will also list alternatives in the event you don't like the way an exercise feels. 
DISCLAIMER!!! If an exercise feels bad or causes pain then DO NOT DO IT! If this
    rule is not followed or is brushed over then there is a risk of serious injury.
I will be detailing a Chest/Back, Arms, and then Legs split.
This split can be modified as one wishes but this is the split I follow. 

A quick little note here! Many if not all of the final sets should be pushed until FAILURE!
This ensures maximum muscle growth though I would not recommend this to a new lifter since they are not fully conditioned.
There will also be lots of stress on the stretched position of the muscle since that is where the most growth happens.
Also, make sure to control the negative as it is scientifically proven to stimulate more growth.

Day 1: Chest/Back (~60-90 min)

Chest:
Machine Chest Fly's: 3x12 (Whole/Middle Chest)
-To perform a maching chest fly you must have acess to a chest fly machine. Alternatively, you can use dumbbells.
1. Set the arm position so that when you are in the bottom of the rep you experience a deep stretch in your chest.
2. Bring your arms arount and to the front of you and perform a big squeeze while they are in front.
3. Do the reps and complete the set while making sure to keep your shoulders tucked and your arms bent.
Incline Bench Press: 3x12 (Upper Chest)
-To perform an incline bench press you can either use a machine or a barbell. I personally prefer the machine but either works!
1. Set the handles to a comfortable position where you can once again feel a stretch.
2. Push the bar or the handles all the way to the top of the range of motion and feel your upper pecs squeeze.
3. Lower the bar slowly in a controlled manner until it hits your upper chest and repeat.
Downward Cable Fly's: 3x12 (Lower Chest)
-To perform a downward cable fly, go to a cable machine with two cables on opposite sides of you and attatch handles to them.
Alternatively, you can do decline bench press.
1. Set the height of the cables to the highest setting to ensure that you get a deep stretch.
2. Grab the handles and pull them down to your waist and squeeze and the bottom. Perform this exercise with the same form as the machine chest fly.
3. Control the weight up to the top and repeat.
Back:
Reverse Fly's: 3x12 (Rear Delts/Middle Traps)
-To perform a reverse fly's you can use a chest fly machine with the arms all the way back and face the machine. Alternatively, you can use dumbbells.
1. Set the arm position so your arms are directly in front of you.
2. Push the bars back as far as you can without swinging and then control the weight down. Repeat.
Neutral Grip Rows: 3x12 (Lats/Mid Back)
-To perform neutral grip rows you must go to a weighted row machine and use a neutral grip attatchment.
1. Grap the grip and push away slightly with your legs making sure to have your back still rouned toward the machine to feel a lat stretch.
2. Pull the grip toward you with your elbows tucked in close to you and feel the squeeze in your lats at the back.
3. Lower the weight slowly until you feel a stretch and repeat.
T-Bar Row: 3x12 (Lats/Mid back)
-To perform a t-bar row, go to a t-bar row apparatus and stand on it with your back straight and chest against the support.
1. Do not allow you back to round! If you do for a deeper stretch, straighten at the end of the rep.
2. Grab the handles and pull them toward your chest while flaring your elbows out slightly. Feel a squeeze in the mid back and lats at the end.
3. Control the weight down and potentially allow your back to round again. Repeat.
Back Extensions: 3x12 (Spinal Erectors)
-To perform back extensions, go to a back extension machine or an apparatus.
1. Allow your back to lower into the hanging position if your using the apparatus. If using the machine, keep your back straight.
2. Without using any momentum, pull your back into an upright position again, if using a machine, push against the pad until your flat.
3. Control your weight back down into the original position and repeat. For the apparatus, weight can be added by using plates.
Leaning Forward Weighted Shrugs: 3x12 (Traps)
-To perform a leading forward weighted shrug you must grab two dumbbells, one in each hand.
1. Tilt your torso forward about 15 degrees from the upright position.
2. Shrug the weight up and back and feel the activation in your upper, lower, and middle traps.
3. Slowly lower the weight and repeat. Be careful not to swing on these since that is very common.


Day 2: Arms (~45-60 min)

Preacher Curls: 3x12 (Biceps)
-To perform a preacher curl, go to a preacher curl apparatus, machine, or grab a bench and put your arm on the bench so that it makes a 45 degree angle with your body.
1. Slowly pull the weight all the way up to your face without swinging your body backward.
2. Slowly lower the weight until it has reacher the bottom. Repeat.
Face-Away Bayesian Cable Curl: 3x12 (Biceps)
-To perform a face away bayesian cable curl you must have a cable with a handle attatchment on it.
1. Set the handle to be just below waist height and stand facing away from the cable. Allow your arm to stretch behind you about 15 degrees.
2. Perform a normal curl pulling the cable toward your shoulder, do not move your upper arm forward and just bend at the elbow.
3. Lower the weight slowly until you feel a deep stretch and repeat.
Zottman hammer curls: 3x12 (biceps and brachioradialis)
-To perform a Zottman hammer curl, grab two dumbbells.
1. Stand up straight and perform a normal hammer curl stopping at the top.
2. Rotate your hands so that your palms are facing upward and lower the weight slowly, you should now feel less forearm and more bicep tension.
3. Repeat while making sure not to use momentum to get the weight up.
Overhead Tricep Extension: 3x12 (Triceps)
-To perform an overhead tricep extension, grab a bar and attatch it to a cable.
1. Set the cable to about waist height and lean forward allowing your tricep to get a big stretch at the bottom of the rep.
2. Push the weight up and over you until its in front of you and slowly lower it back to the stretch. Repeat.
Tricep Pressdown: 3x12 (Triceps)
-To perform a tricep pressdown, grab a bar and attatch it to a cable.
1. Set the cable to its highest height and gram the bar with a sturdy, upright, posture.
2. While keeping your elbows tucked and at your side, push the weight down toward the floor.
3. Slowly let the weight back up and repeat. Try not to use bodyweight as momentum to help.
Shoulder Press: 3x12 (Side Delts/Front Delts)
-To perform a shoulder press, go to a shoulder press machine. Alternatively, you can use dumbbells.
1. Sit upright on a bench and have the handles or the weights in a spot where your shoulder is stretched.
2. Push the weight up all the way above you and slowly let it down. Make sure to not let your elbows too far either way. Repeat.
Cable Lateral Raises: 3x12 (Side Delts)
-To perform a cable lateral raise go to a cable machine and attatch a handle.
1. Set the cable at waist height and stand about 1 or 2 feet away from the cable.
2. Raise the weight up and outward making sure to feel it in your shoulder, go until arm is parallel with the floor.
3. Slowly lower the weight and repeat. Make sure to cross over your body slightly to get a deeper stretch.
Reverse Cable Fly's: 3x12 (Rear Delts)
-To perform a reverse cable fly set the cable to the lowest height and grab the ball in which you would normally put an attatchment.
1. Have a stiff posture and position your torso to be parallel to the floor.
2. Pull the cable behind you all the way to be parallel with the floor as well.
3. Slowly let the weight back down and feel the deep stretch on the back part of your shoulder.
Forearm Curls: 3x20 (Forearm Flexors)
-To perform a forearm curl, go to a cable machine and put the bar at the highest height.
1. Grab onto the bar with your palms facing down and curl the weight downward only rotating from your wrist.
2. Try not to use too much momentum and repeat for 20 reps, do as many as possible on last set.


Day 3: Legs (~60-90 min)

Seated Hamstring Curls: 3x12 (Hamstrings)
-To perform a seated hamstring curl, find a seated hamstring curl machine.
1. Put the seat forward so that you are in a comfortable position and the leg brace is tight on you.
2. Curl the weight down and allow it to go back up slowly. Repeat.
Barbell Back Squats: 3x8-12 (Quads/Glutes)
-To perform a barbell back squat find a squat rack with a barbell on it and weights.
1. Put the barbell on your traps and go squat down keeping your back straight and hinging from your hips until your calves and thighs make a right angle.
2. Push off the floor like your standing up and push your butt back out and round onto the top.
3. Make sure that if you are doing 8 reps rather than twelve, make them count and be worth more than just normal reps.
Romanian Deadlifts: 3x12 (Hamstrings/Spinal Erectors)
-To perform a RDL, move the bar to the floor.
1. Allow your knees to bend slightly and hinge from your hips again. Lower your back and grab the weight while keeping your back straight.
2. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings and drive through your hamstrings/hips to get the weight up and repeat. Kinda like a deadlift.
Angled Leg Press: 3x12 (Quads)
-To perform an angled leg press, go to an angled leg press machine.
1. Sit in the leg press machine and unlock the press. Allow it to go down until your calves and thighs make a right angle.
2. Press the weight back up through your feet and allow yourself to use your hands to push on your knees if needed. Repeat.
Leg Extensions: 3x12 (Quads)
-To perform a leg extension, find a leg extension machine.
1. Set the pad angle so your legs stretch at the bottom. Set the seat far back to experience even more stretch and put the pad just below the knees.
2. Push against the pad and squeeze with your quads at the top.
3. Slowly let the weight back down and repeat. Try not to use bodyweight as momentum and try not to swing.
Hip Thrusts: 3x12 (Glutes)
-To perform a hip thrust, go to a smith machine and put the bar low with a pad for less pressure.
1. Lay your back on a bench and put your knees just a bit wider than shoulder width.
2. Let the weight fall and then drive through your hips and through your feet feeling a squeeze in your glutes at the top.
Calf Raises: 3x15 (Calves)
-To perform a calf raise you can either do them seated or standing.
1. Allow your calves to fall below the flat part of your foot making them into a stretched position.
2. Raise the calves up and feel them tighten as you go onto your tip toes. Now drop and repeat.
